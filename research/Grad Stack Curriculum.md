Grad Stack - Structured academic curriculum

### **The "Grad Stack" Enterprise Syllabus**

**Course Code:** GRAD2026 (Example)
**Total Modules:** 5
**Est. Completion Time:** 2–4 Hours (Self-Paced)
**Learning Focus:** Graduate Employability & Industry Transition

---

### **Module 1: Market Intelligence & Strategic Alignment**

**Academic Goal:** *Students will understand the macro-recruitment landscape and align their personal brand with current industry expectations.*

* **1.1 The Macro View:** Overview of the Graduate Program Process
* **1.2 The Evaluator’s Perspective:** The Mindset of the Hiring Manager
* **1.3 Industry Insight (Masterclass):** Interview with a Current Grad Program Manager
* **1.4 Peer Learning Case Study:** Interview with a Recent Grad Program Participant
* **1.5 Network Architecture:** Networking Strategies for Early Career
* **1.6 Digital Branding:** Optimizing the LinkedIn Profile

> **Why this works for Uni:** It moves beyond "tips" to "strategic understanding." It shows you are teaching students *how the market works*, not just how to fill in a form.

---

### **Module 2: Digital Gatekeepers & Documentation**

**Academic Goal:** *Students will develop technical proficiency in navigating AI-driven recruitment filters (ATS) and produce professional-grade application documentation.*

* **2.1 The AI Barrier:** Interview: Insights from an Applicant Tracking System (ATS) Expert
* **2.2 Document Creation:** High-Impact Resumes (Optimization for Human & Machine)
* **2.3 Narrative Strategy:** Writing the Cover Letter
* **2.4 Psychometric Proficiency (Theory):** Understanding Psychometric Testing
* **2.5 Psychometric Insight (Masterclass):** Interview: Insights from an Expert in Psychometric Testing

> **Why this works for Uni:** This addresses the "Digital Literacy" GLO (Graduate Learning Outcome). It positions your product as a solution to the "AI screening" threat.

---

### **Module 3: Asynchronous Communication (The Digital Interview)**

**Academic Goal:** *Students will demonstrate adaptability and communication skills in remote and automated assessment environments.*

* **3.1 The Screening Phase:** Navigating Phone Screenings
* **3.2 Asynchronous Strategy:** Mastering Automated (One-Way) Interviews
* **3.3 Digital Presence (Masterclass):** Interview: Insights from an Expert in Automated Video Interviews

> **Why this works for Uni:** Universities struggle to teach this. Traditional role-play doesn't work for "talking to a camera." This module fills a specific gap in their current curriculum.

---

### **Module 4: Synchronous Interview Mastery**

**Academic Goal:** *Students will articulate their value proposition effectively in high-pressure, live interview scenarios using behavioral frameworks.*

* **4.1 The Interview Framework:** Overview of Job Interviews & Types
* **4.2 Non-Verbal Communication:** Creating the Right First Impression
* **4.3 Rapport Building:** Building Connection with Interviewers
* **4.4 Structuring Responses:** 5 Big Tips for Memorable Answers (STAR Method)
* **4.5 The "About You" Component:** Tackling Personal and Career Questions
* **4.6 Evidence-Based Answers:** Responding to Behavioural Questions
* **4.7 Domain Expertise:** Responding to Technical Questions
* **4.8 Complex Scenarios:** Navigating Tricky Questions
* **4.9 Strategic Engagement:** Asking Insightful Questions

> **Why this works for Uni:** This is the core "Communication Skills" module. It is comprehensive and structured, covering soft skills (rapport) and hard skills (technical questions).

---

### **Module 5: Advanced Selection & Professional Resilience**

**Academic Goal:** *Students will prepare for multi-stakeholder assessments and develop the psychological resilience required for the competitive job market.*

* **5.1 Group Dynamics (Masterclass):** Interview: Insights from an Expert in Assessment Centres
* **5.2 Verification:** Managing Reference Checks
* **5.3 Psychological Safety:** Resilience and Adaptability
* **5.4 Career Planning:** Next Steps (The Offer or The Rejection)

> **Why this works for Uni:** "Resilience" is a buzzword in higher education right now. Ending the course on mental health and adaptability is a major selling point for Student Experience teams.
